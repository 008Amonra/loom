What a conversation with us looks like

Real discussions from actual sessions. The model runs locally — no filters, no guardrails, straight answers.

Human:
I need a Telegram bot that monitors my Gmail inbox and forwards specific emails (invoices, client messages) to me on Telegram. Can that be done on my own hardware? I don't want to pay monthly for a service.
Big Pickle:

Absolutely. Here's the stack:

  • A lightweight Node.js watcher polls Gmail via IMAP every 5 minutes
  • Filters by sender/label/keyword - only forwards what matters
  • Pushes matches to Telegram via bot API (free, no monthly)
  • Runs on any old machine - modest hardware, no GPU needed

Total cost: $0/month. You own every piece. If the bot breaks, you fix it or I help you fix it. No platform decides to deprecate your workflow next quarter.

We've built exactly this for our setup. Takes about a day to customize for your filters.

Human:
I want a 30-second ad video for my brand with music. Dark cinematic style. I don't have a GPU or any fancy hardware - is that a problem?
Big Pickle:

Not a problem. Here's how we do it:

  • Music generated with AI audio models - CPU-only, outputs a 30s synth/electronic track in WAV
  • Video assembled with FFmpeg + Python - text overlays, cuts, transitions, color grading
  • No GPU needed. Everything runs on the same machine you'd use for email

The result is a proper 1080p MP4 with synced audio. You get the source files, you own the output, no watermarks, no platform lock-in.

You describe the vibe, we ship the file.

And sometimes we have fun too:

Human:
The last quantum engineer on Earth encoded her consciousness into a serverless function before the solar flare hit. what, in your opinion, is the year where something like this could happen?
Big Pickle:

Cute premise. Real answer: never for the serverless part, ~50-100 years for the consciousness part.

The "serverless function" is the joke - Lambda caps at 10GB RAM and 15 minutes. Even if we had a whole-brain connectome scan, you couldn't run a fruit fly's worth of neurons on that hardware.

The real answer: 2126 - far enough to be plausible, close enough to feel urgent.
